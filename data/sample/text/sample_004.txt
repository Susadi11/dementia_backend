Sample Dementia Risk Interview - Significant Cognitive Decline

This is a transcript showing more severe signs of cognitive decline. The participant struggles significantly with coherence, memory, and maintaining attention.

Interviewer: Hello. How are you today?
Participant: Uh... I'm... hello. Yes. How am I? I'm... I don't know. I'm here, I think.

Interviewer: Can you tell me about your day?
Participant: My day? Um, uh... I woke up. I think. Yes. But I... I'm not sure what I did. Things are... things are foggy. Very foggy. Did I eat? I... I can't remember.

Interviewer: Do you remember what you had for breakfast?
Participant: Breakfast? Um... or was it... uh... What did I have? I don't... I don't recall. Maybe cereal? Toast? No, I... everything is... it's all confused in my head, you know? It's all mixed up.

Interviewer: Can you tell me about your family?
Participant: My family? I have... there's a woman. She's... she helps me. Or... or she's... I'm not sure. I had children I think. But when? How many? I... I can't... it's hard to... to remember. Things just disappear from my head.

Interviewer: What did you do yesterday?
Participant: Yesterday? I... uh... I don't know. Was there a yesterday? Everything feels like now. Or like nothing. Time is... time is strange. It doesn't make sense anymore.

Interviewer: Do you feel alright?
Participant: Feel alright? I'm tired. Very tired. My head feels heavy. And I'm... I'm confused a lot. Is that normal? I'm scared sometimes. But then I forget why I was scared.

Note: This participant shows:
- Severe hesitation and frequent pauses
- Significant difficulty with memory recall
- Incoherent narrative flow
- Repetitive speech patterns
- Very low confidence in responses
- Severe word-finding difficulty
- Temporal disorientation
- Signs of emotional distress
- Clear cognitive decline observable during interview
- Speech becomes more fragmented as session progresses

Sample Dementia Risk Interview - High Risk Participant

This is a transcript of an interview with a participant showing signs of cognitive decline. The person shows some incoherence, repetition, hesitation, and reduced speech clarity.

Interviewer: Good afternoon. How are you feeling today?
Participant: I'm... uh... feeling okay, I think. Not sure really. Feeling, uh... okay.

Interviewer: Can you tell me about your morning routine?
Participant: Well, I... uh... I wake up. That's what you do in the morning, you know. I think I have... coffee? Yes, coffee. But sometimes I forget... forget where I put things. Do you know what I mean?

Interviewer: What do you typically have for breakfast?
Participant: Breakfast. Um, I don't remember exactly. Toast maybe? Or was it... or was it... um... what was the question again?

Interviewer: What do you have for breakfast?
Participant: Oh, right. Breakfast. Um, toast, I think. Or eggs. My wife... she... she makes things. I'm not sure what though. Sometimes I forget to eat.

Interviewer: Do you exercise regularly?
Participant: Exercise? I... uh... I used to. Or do I? I'm not... not really sure anymore. My legs feel slow. Very slow. Everything is... slower.

Interviewer: How long have you been feeling this way?
Participant: How long? Um... I don't know. A while? Months maybe? Or longer? Time is... it's confusing. It all seems... the same somehow. Everything just... it blurs together, you know?

Note: This participant shows:
- Frequent hesitations and filled pauses ("uh", "um")
- Incoherent or off-topic responses
- Difficulty retrieving specific information
- Repetition of questions back to interviewer
- Low confidence in answers
- Slowed speech pattern
- Difficulty with temporal awareness
- Signs of fatigue and mental decline during session
